2, Market direction (cont.)
Three questions IDV must answer today
1
Is this entity real?
Liveness detection · injection attack detection · behavioral signals
  • ·Liveness is table stakes. Every vendor has it.
  • ·The gap opens with injection attacks: virtual cameras feeding pre-recorded footage directly into the capture pipeline.
  • ·Detecting injections requires a layered approach: device signals, behavioral context, and network telemetry.
2
Is this person who they claim to be?
Biometric match · authoritative source verification · document authenticity
  • ·95% of synthetic identities go undetected during onboarding. (Thomson Reuters)
  • ·Data and document checks are not enough.
  • ·Deterministic verification matches against biometric records directly. Users cannot manipulate a system of record.
3
Can we trust this identity over time?
Cross-institution signals · first-party fraud · lifecycle monitoring · network intelligence
  • ·Identity signals need to stay connected after onboarding — a 360-degree view of the user across their lifecycle.
  • ·Account takeover, synthetic identity reuse across institutions, and first-party fraud from verified customers are all invisible without cross-institutional signal sharing.

11 — Agentic identity
Securing the agentic internet
AI agents are executing real transactions today. The identity infrastructure to govern them doesn't exist at scale yet.
Why agents break existing IDV
  • ·Agents generate no useful telemetry — they run from identical infrastructure with identical behavioral patterns.
  • ·The only durable signal is the verified link to the human behind them.
The identity challenge this creates
  • ·An agent cannot present a face or a document. It must present proof that a verified human explicitly authorized it, within a defined scope.
  • ·Biometrics is the only gatekeeping mechanism agents cannot replicate.
Incode's position
  • ·Building the agentic identity layer on top of existing deployed infrastructure — no greenfield build required.
  • ·When an agent attempts a high-sensitivity action, verification routes to the human principal. The human is always the root of trust.
Incode's agentic architecture
Detect
Identify agentic sessions on human-facing surfaces. Distinguish between agent and human traffic in real time.
Bind
Link the agent to a verified human owner via biometric confirmation. Scoped credential issued for authorized actions only.
Monitor
Use Trust Graph to continuously monitor signals associated with the human owner. Human and agentic activity linked in a single identity record.
Audit
Full chain of custody for every agent-executed transaction. Compatible with MCP, A2A, and open protocols.
